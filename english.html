The awareness campaigns for each month.

April

Autism awareness

Blue

Blue April is celebrated every year as the month dedicated to autism awareness. During this period, landmarks in Brazil and around the world are illuminated in blue, the color that symbolizes Autism Spectrum Disorder. Various campaigns aim to share information, combat prejudice, and encourage inclusive attitudes in everyday life. Autism is characterized by different forms of communication, behavior, and social interaction. Because it is an invisible disability, it is often not noticeable at first glance, which leads to the mistaken and ableist idea that an autistic person “doesn’t look autistic.” Autism is a spectrum. Each autistic person expresses their characteristics in a unique way. No one “looks autistic”. They simply are.

May

Road safety

Yellow

Yellow May is an international awareness movement that aims to reduce accidents and promote road safety. Created in 2014, the movement seeks to draw society’s attention to the high number of deaths and injuries on the roads, encouraging respect for traffic laws and responsible behavior. Throughout the month of May, public agencies, schools, companies, and organizations hold lectures, campaigns, and educational actions focused on prevention. The movement reinforces that road safety is everyone’s responsibility: pedestrians, cyclists, motorcyclists, and drivers alike. By adopting safe behaviors—such as not using a cellphone while driving, respecting speed limits, and wearing a seat belt—each person contributes to saving lives and making traffic more humane and aware.

June

Blood donation

Red

Red June is a national campaign created to encourage blood donation, especially because winter often leads to a decrease in the number of donors. The month is marked by mobilization efforts in blood centers and healthcare institutions. A single donation can save up to four lives, as the collected blood is separated into different components, such as platelets, plasma, and red blood cells. These elements are essential for treating patients with cancer, severe anemia, accident victims, and people undergoing surgery. Donating blood is a voluntary, compassionate, and safe act. The entire process is carried out with disposable materials and qualified professionals. Participating in this campaign is a gesture of empathy and citizenship, showing how small individual actions can lead to significant transformations in society.

The meaning of the colors and the reason they were chosen.

Blue

This cool color is associated with the mind and intellect. It gives a sense of calm and tranquility, evoking the sea, water, and the night. Blue is the color of wisdom, integrity, and knowledge; it enhances creativity and clarity of thought, bringing a sense of safety and trust. In lighter shades, it works well with themes aimed at children. In darker tones, it is widely used in business and education.
The color blue was chosen as the symbol of the campaign, representing tranquility and the need for understanding in relation to autism.

Yellow

Yellow is the color of the sun and happiness, always full of energy.
On one hand, it is a joyful and dynamic color that symbolizes celebration. On the other, it is associated with the intellectual side of the mind and the ability to express thoughts. It is also linked to gold, money, and luxury. Additionally, in nature, yellow often signals danger, as seen in wasps or bees.
The color yellow was selected for the campaign because it symbolizes attention, alertness, and warning, connecting to traffic signs.

Red

Red symbolizes a range of intense and powerful emotions, both positive and negative. It is associated with passion, love, blood, strength, energy, and life. Practically, red is used to draw attention, create a sense of urgency, stimulate appetite, and represent power or warning. It is the color of the element fire, of blood, and of the human heart.
Red represents the blood that saves lives, and the initiative seeks to encourage donation to increase supplies in blood centers. For this reason, it was chosen as the color for the month of June.

connection with the SDGs

Green April seeks to prevent workplace accidents and occupational illnesses, ensuring safer and healthier work environments — one of the pillars of SDG 3. Blue April, in turn, contributes by raising awareness about autism, encouraging early diagnosis, access to proper healthcare services, and social inclusion. This reinforces SDG 3’s commitment to mental health.

Green April

Green April is a campaign focused on preventing accidents and illnesses related to the work environment. The initiative aims to promote care-oriented actions and encourage safe practices in the daily routines of companies and workers. The movement originated in 1968, after the tragic Farmington Mine Disaster in the United States, which resulted in the deaths of 78 workers. In 2003, the International Labour Organization (ILO) established April 28 as the World Day for Safety and Health at Work, in honor of the victims of that accident. According to data from SmartLab — the Occupational Safety and Health Observatory — Brazil is among the countries with the highest number of workplace accidents. Every 51 seconds an accident occurs, and every four hours someone dies as a result. Green April encourages the creation of a safety culture that goes beyond simply complying with regulatory standards. Promoting prevention is essential to ensuring workers’ well-being and reducing costs related to absences and compensation.

connection with the SDGs.

The “iridescent may ” campaign connects to the third SDG because it promotes care, prevention, and support for maternal mental health, recognizing that the well-being of mothers is essential for the health of the entire family.The campaign expands access to information, combats stigma, and encourages policies and practices that ensure health and well-being for all, as proposed by the third SDG.

iridescent may

iridescent may is a campaign that aims to bring visibility to maternal mental health. The name symbolizes the idea that, just as an iridescent surface reveals multiple colors as the light shifts, motherhood is also complex and shaped by many emotions: joy, love, exhaustion, fear, guilt, and vulnerability. The campaign highlights how many women face intense social pressure to be “perfect,” deal with physical and mental overload, and often silence their own suffering. Conditions such as postpartum depression, anxiety, maternal exhaustion, and parental burnout are common, yet still surrounded by stigma. By promoting information, welcoming support, and building a network of care, iridescent may reinforces that caring for maternal mental health is not a luxury, but a necessity. The initiative encourages mothers to be heard, to seek help without guilt, and calls on society to take responsibility in supporting the women who care for, raise, and sustain so many lives.

Green June

Green June is a month entirely dedicated to environmental awareness and protection, emphasizing the importance of rethinking habits and strengthening sustainable actions in daily life. The campaign invites society to reflect on the impact of human activities on nature, highlighting themes such as biodiversity preservation, pollution reduction, combating deforestation, and encouraging the circular economy. Throughout the month, schools, companies, public institutions, and communities often promote educational initiatives, tree planting, cleanup efforts, discussions on climate change, and actions aimed at the responsible use of natural resources.More than a symbolic observance, Green June reinforces that protecting the environment is essential to ensuring quality of life, health, and balance for future generations.
